ABOUT

Second Slice Café began in Northcote in 2015 as a small family owned café.

Owners Trevor and Naomi were passionate about healthy food and spreading health throughout their communities. The Second Slice’s menu is always evolving to focus on nutrition without compromising on quality or taste. The café is embracing sustainability and has a “Zero Waste” policy for operation.

After three years of learning, growing, and serving the Northcote community with fresh, organic foods, Juicy Ladies has decided to go online to let more customer know about them. They have established an online ordering system to be able to provide their customers with fast services. Their focus continues to be centred around spreading knowledge of and access to nutritious foods as well as supporting local small business.

ZERO WASTE

"Zero waste, if correctly implemented, will eliminate toxic waste that poses a threat to our entire earth, including the people on it. Rather than being a form of waste management, we see it as a form of waste prevention with the goal of completely changing the way our communities use and reuse our resources to result in a world free of unused and discarded materials."

Table

  • Work more with the seasons and offer seasonal specials
  • Offer small and regular portion sizes to reduce leftover food
  • Advise customers if they maybe over ordering too much food
  • Avoid single-use items, provide reusable/recyclable stirrers, spoons, straws etc
  • Avoid using single use sachets for condiments

Kitchen

  • Order fresh ingredients in small batches and dry ingredients in bulk
  • Use all the parts of the fruit and vegetables
  • Purchase products with minimal packaging and that is recycled locally
  • Offer more drinks which do not require the use of straws
  • Source more ingredients from local organic farmers/suppliers and avoid airfreighting where possible

Community

  • Promote taking leftovers home
  • Offer eco friendly bags/containers for takeaways
  • Share our eco actions on social media and incentivise your customers to do the same
  • Collect and upcycle your cooking oil into either soap or sell it for Bio-diesel use
  • Offer a return deposit scheme on your glass containers
